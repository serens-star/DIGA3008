My Experience Setting Up GitHub

My experience setting up my GitHub was relatively easy, seeing as I already have a GitHub account and I have experience committing my work onto a repository from last year. What I did have trouble with was adapting that knowledge to a new software like Visual Studio Code. Though we did get a step by step guide on how to get Visual Studio Code set up, I did feel out of my depth trying to get used to how this software works, and getting familiar with the new coding language and its layout. Having a tutor walk me through the whole process again and showing me where I went wrong, definitely made the whole process a lot easier and faster. I feel a lot more comfortable with committing my changes for VSCode to Github now.

Reflecting on the Readings

One of the readings I had selected was “Flash and its History on the Web” by Jay. This article was particularly very interesting, to me, as someone who’s been on the internet for as long as I can remember. I did not expect to be so enthralled in the history of the internet, especially when the article was about a random software platform I’ve never heard of before. Upon reading it further, I came to realise how much of what piqued my interest, as a child and even as an adult, about the internet and technology itself was somehow connected to Flash’s software platform. The article mentioned how FutureWave’s (Flash) ‘first target was [...] computers you could operate with a pen or stylus’ and that brought me back to when I was introduced to a stylus for the first time. This was probably the early 2010’s because I could have sworn my mom still had her Blackberry; I had just seen a commercial on the TV about a brand new phone with a stylus and I instantly fell in love. After months of begging my parents to get me one, they eventually caved and, I would not put that stylus down, it was like the best thing to ever exist, as far as I was concerned. I grew up and my sentiment has not changed about the stylus. It is quite interesting how time periods play a role in the success of technology because after FutureWave took 2 years to build out SmartSketch and the pen computer, it had lost all its popularity by 1995, but 10 years later, other software companies are gaining popularity from what Flash had started.

Interesting Things Found on the History of the Web

An interesting thing I learnt about the history of the internet was actually from this article. I learnt that so much of the internet, that we know today, was connected to Flash’s multimedia software platform. Knowing how Flash’s development was practically built in tandem with the web is very surprising to me, seeing as I’ve never heard about Flash and its influence on the internet in the late 90’s to early 2000’s , until right now. Something I found particularly fascinating was the fact that YouTube used to be a dating site. The fact that Rabois (an investor for Karim’s own video uploading and streaming service) had to “convince[...] the YouTube team to drop their silly dating site pretenses and focus on [...] uploading and streaming videos.” is especially funny to me.

Goals For the Course

I have a couple of goals for this course. One being for my website portfolio to be an extension of my creativity. I want whatever vision I have, to be well executed to its fullest extent. Another goal of mine is to stay on top of the coursework, and not fall behind to the point where I’m rushing to do things at the last minute. The last thing I want is to have my work be rushed and for my intended vision to fall through because of my bad time management. I would also like to become a better writer and for my writing to be engaging. I want to articulate myself better, be able to unpack interesting topics in a way that shows I have understood the topic being discussed and for me to confidently make my points.
